Japanese Red Cross

Relief Organization

Tokyo, Japan

Founded in 1877 as the Philanthropic Society, by Count Tsunetami Sano, to relieve the injured of the Satsuma Rebellion. In 1887, the organization changed its name to the Japanese Red Cross Society, officialy recognized by the International Committee of the Red Cross on September 2. Today, they operate 92 hospitals and 79 blood centers throughout Japan.
